Know Your Plant

Comprehensive plant database with pesticide recommendations

Important Safety Guidelines

Personal Protection

Always wear protective equipment including masks, gloves, and appropriate clothing when handling pesticides.

Application Timing

Apply pesticides during early morning or late evening to avoid harming beneficial insects and reduce evaporation.

Weather Conditions

Avoid application during windy conditions (>10 mph) or when rain is expected within 24 hours.

Proper Dosage

Always follow the recommended dosage. More is not better and can harm plants and the environment.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM is an environmentally sensitive approach to pest management that relies on multiple techniques:

Monitoring

Regular inspection of crops to identify pest problems early and determine when treatment is needed.

Prevention

Using resistant varieties, proper sanitation, and cultural practices to prevent pest problems.

Biological Control

Encouraging natural enemies of pests through habitat management and beneficial insect releases.

Targeted Treatment

Using pesticides only when necessary and selecting products that are least harmful to beneficial insects.

Sustainable Farming Practices

Water Management

Use drip irrigation and mulching to conserve water and reduce pesticide runoff.

Crop Rotation

Rotate crops to break pest cycles and maintain soil health naturally.

Cover Crops

Plant cover crops to improve soil structure, suppress weeds, and provide habitat for beneficial insects.

Data-Driven Decisions

Use IoT sensors and data analytics to make informed decisions about irrigation and pest management.
